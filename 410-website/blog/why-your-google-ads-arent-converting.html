Why Your Google Ads Aren't Converting (And What to Actually Fix)

By 410 Strategy · April 2, 2026 · 8 min read

You set up Google Ads. You're spending money. Traffic is coming in. But the leads aren't there — or the ones that are coming in aren't closing.

This is one of the most common situations we see when a new client brings us their account. And here's what we've learned: the budget is almost never the problem. The fix is almost always structural.

Here are the five conversion killers we find most often — and how to address each one.

76%

of Google Ads accounts we audit have at least 3 of these 5 issues active simultaneously.

The 5 Conversion Killers

  • 1
    Broad Match Keywords Without Negative Lists

    Broad match lets Google decide who sees your ads. Without a robust negative keyword list, you'll burn budget on irrelevant searches — "free," "jobs," "how to," and competitors' brand terms are common culprits. We've audited accounts spending $5,000/month where 40% of clicks were completely non-converting search terms.

    The Fix

    Pull your Search Terms report. Any term that doesn't reflect buying intent — add it to your negative list. Do this every week without exception. Over time, your budget concentrates on the searches that actually convert.

  • 2
    Landing Page Doesn't Match Search Intent

    Someone searches "emergency plumber Billings MT" and your ad sends them to your homepage. That's a conversion killer. The message match is broken — they came with urgency and you greeted them with your company history. Google measures this mismatch through Quality Score and you pay for it in higher CPCs.

    The Fix

    Create dedicated landing pages for each ad group. The headline should use the searcher's exact language. The CTA should match the urgency of the query. One clear action, nothing else. This alone often doubles conversion rates.

  • 3
    Conversion Tracking Is Broken or Missing

    This one is rampant. Google can't optimize toward conversions it can't see. If your tracking isn't firing correctly — or you're optimizing for "page views" instead of "form submissions" or "calls" — the entire Smart Bidding system is flying blind. You're essentially paying for a GPS with no map loaded.

    The Fix

    Verify every conversion action in Google Ads → Tools → Conversions. Confirm they're firing with Google Tag Assistant. Set your primary conversion to the deepest action (form fill, call, purchase). Secondary conversions can include softer signals.

  • 4
    Ad Copy Sells Features, Not Outcomes

    "Professional, licensed, insured plumbing services" doesn't convert. "Fast Hot Water Restored Today — Call Now" does. Your prospects don't buy features. They buy relief, results, and certainty. Most ads are written from the seller's perspective. Write from the buyer's.

    The Fix

    For every headline, ask: "Would a person with this problem care about this?" If the answer is "maybe," cut it. Lead with the outcome they want. Include social proof ("100+ Reviews, 4.9 Stars"), urgency ("Same-Day Service"), and a clear next step.

  • 5
    You're Bidding on the Wrong Campaign Type

    Performance Max campaigns have their place — but for service businesses that need local leads, Search campaigns with tight keyword control often outperform PMax significantly. Similarly, Display and YouTube burn budget without clear intent signals. Many business owners let Google push them into campaign types that benefit Google's revenue, not theirs.

    The Fix

    For most local service businesses, start with Search only. Add location targeting radius. Use phrase and exact match. Control your own budget allocation before trusting Google to do it for you. Once you have conversion data, then explore other campaign types.

The Common Thread

Every one of these issues has the same root cause: the account was set up once and never actively managed. Google's defaults favor Google's revenue. Smart Bidding needs clean conversion data. Search Terms reports need weekly attention.

Ads that convert aren't set-and-forget. They're built, tested, refined, and optimized every week. That's the gap between businesses that see 2× ROAS and those seeing 4–6×.
