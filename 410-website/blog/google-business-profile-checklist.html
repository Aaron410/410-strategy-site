The 10-Point Google Business Profile Checklist Every Local Business Needs

By 410 Strategy · April 2, 2026 · 7 min read

Here's something most local businesses don't realize: your Google Business Profile is probably your highest-traffic marketing asset — and it's free. More people will find you through Google Maps and the local pack than through your website, your ads, or your social profiles combined.

The problem? Most profiles are incomplete, miscategorized, or missing key trust signals that Google uses to decide who ranks. We audit GBPs every week for clients across Montana and nationally. The same 10 issues come up over and over.

Run through this checklist. Fix what's broken. You'll see movement within weeks.

The 10-Point Checklist

  • 1
    Business Name Matches Exactly Your GBP name should match your real-world business name — nothing more. No keyword stuffing ("Joe's HVAC Best Billings Montana"). Google penalizes this. If your sign says "Apex Plumbing," your GBP should say "Apex Plumbing."
  • 2
    Primary Category Is Exactly Right This is the single biggest ranking factor most businesses get wrong. Don't pick "General Contractor" when you should pick "Kitchen Remodeler." Google gives each category its own ranking weight. Spend 10 minutes researching what your top local competitors use.
  • 3
    Secondary Categories Are Filled In You can add up to 9 additional categories. These expand your ranking footprint. A med spa might use Primary: Medical Spa, Secondary: Laser Hair Removal Service, Skin Care Clinic, Botox Clinic. Each secondary category is a potential ranking opportunity.
  • 4
    Services Section Is Complete Under "Services," you can add every specific service with its own name, description, and price (optional). This is keyword-rich content that Google reads and indexes. Most businesses leave this blank. Fill in every service you offer — 15 to 30 is not too many.
  • 5
    Business Description Uses Real Keywords You get 750 characters. Use them. Write naturally, but include your primary service, your city, and the type of customer you serve. Avoid fluff. "We've served Billings families with quality plumbing since 2008" beats "Welcome to our company!"
  • 6
    Photos Are Recent and Plentiful Google tracks photo activity. Profiles with 100+ photos get significantly more calls and direction requests than profiles with 10. Add photos of your team, your work, your location. Set a reminder to upload 5 new photos every month.
  • 7
    Google Posts Are Active Most businesses post once and forget about it. Google Posts (under "Add Update") are indexed by Google and show in your profile. Post weekly — promotions, new services, seasonal content. Fresh activity signals to Google that your business is active.
  • 8
    Q&A Section Is Seeded Anyone can add questions to your GBP — and Google will answer them with AI if you don't. Beat them to it. Add the 5-10 questions customers ask most often, then answer them yourself. This is free real estate on your profile.
  • 9
    NAP Is Consistent Everywhere Name, Address, Phone (NAP) must match exactly across your GBP, website, Yelp, Facebook, and every directory you're listed in. Inconsistencies confuse Google and lower your local ranking. Even "St." vs "Street" can matter.
  • 10
    You're Responding to Every Review Response rate and recency both factor into local rankings. Respond to every review — positive and negative — within 24 hours. For negative reviews, be professional, offer to resolve offline, and never argue. Google watches this.

How Do You Score?

If you checked off all 10, your GBP is in solid shape. If you found 3 or more gaps, you're likely losing leads to competitors who rank above you because their profile is simply more complete.

The good news: this is fixable. These are not technical problems. They're content and configuration problems — and they're usually correctable in a single afternoon.
