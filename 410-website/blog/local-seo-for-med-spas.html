Local SEO for Med Spas: How to Show Up When Clients Search

By 410 Strategy · April 2, 2026 · 9 min read

The med spa industry is one of the fastest-growing segments in aesthetics. The American Med Spa Association estimates the market is growing at 12–15% annually — and with that growth comes a surge in new competitors fighting for the same local search rankings you need.

Here's the reality: most clients don't search for a specific med spa brand. They search for services. "Botox near me." "Lip filler [city]." "Best laser hair removal [neighborhood]." They click the first credible result they see. If that's not you, it's your competitor.

Local SEO for med spas is a discipline that combines Google Business Profile optimization, website authority, reputation management, and service-specific content. Here's how to build a presence that dominates local search for the treatments that drive your revenue.

1. Get Your Google Business Profile Right First

Your GBP is your most valuable local SEO asset — and for med spas, the category selection is everything. Most practices pick one or two categories and leave it there. That's a ranking opportunity missed.

Recommended GBP Categories for Med Spas

  • Primary: Medical Spa
  • Laser Hair Removal Service
  • Skin Care Clinic
  • Botox Clinic (if applicable)
  • Day Spa
  • Health Spa
  • Beauty Salon (secondary, for general visibility)

Each additional category expands your Google Maps ranking surface for those terms. You can add up to 9 categories total.

Beyond categories, fill in every service with its own description. Google indexes this content. If someone searches "CoolSculpting near me" and your Services section mentions CoolSculpting with a description, you'll rank for it. If it's blank, you won't.

Read our full 10-point GBP checklist for a complete optimization walkthrough.

2. Target the Right Keywords for Your Services

Med spa clients search with high intent. They know what treatment they want. Your job is to rank for the exact phrases they type in — and those phrases follow predictable patterns.

Service High-Intent Search Patterns
Botox "Botox [city]", "botox injections near me", "how much does botox cost [city]"
Laser Hair Removal "laser hair removal [city]", "best laser hair removal near me", "[body part] laser [city]"
Lip Filler "lip filler [city]", "dermal fillers near me", "lip injections [city]"
Skin Tightening "skin tightening near me", "RF microneedling [city]", "non-surgical facelift [city]"
Body Contouring "CoolSculpting near me", "body contouring [city]", "fat reduction treatment [city]"

Each of these should have a dedicated landing page on your website — not just a mention on your services page. Google ranks pages, not websites. More dedicated pages = more ranking opportunities.

3. Build Reviews That Work For You

For med spas, reviews are social proof for a high-trust purchase decision. Clients are considering injecting something into their face or using a laser on their body — they're reading reviews carefully. But reviews also directly influence your Google Maps ranking.

The formula that works: Volume + recency + responses. Aim for at least 2 new reviews per week, respond to every review within 24 hours, and train your front desk staff to ask at checkout. A simple "If you enjoyed your experience, a Google review really helps us" at the right moment drives more reviews than any automated follow-up.

For negative reviews: respond professionally, offer to resolve privately, and never argue. One graceful response to a negative review can actually increase trust — it shows you're attentive and accountable.

4. Create Service-Specific Content

This is where most med spas leave significant ranking potential untapped. Blog posts and service pages that answer the exact questions your future clients are searching can drive consistent organic traffic — and they compound over time.

Content Ideas That Rank for Med Spas

  • "How many Botox units do I need for [area]?"
  • "Botox vs. Dysport: What's the difference?"
  • "What to expect during your first laser hair removal session"
  • "How long does lip filler last?"
  • "CoolSculpting vs. Liposuction: which is right for you?"
  • "Preparing for your first microneedling appointment"
  • "[City] med spa guide: what to look for when choosing a provider"

These aren't blog posts for the sake of blogging. Each one targets a specific search that real clients type in when they're researching a purchase decision.

5. Get Listed Everywhere Clients Look

Beyond Google, your potential clients are searching on Yelp, RealSelf, Healthgrades, and Facebook. These platforms also influence your Google ranking — citations (mentions of your business name, address, and phone) across credible directories build your local authority.

Priorities for med spas:

  • RealSelf — high-intent aesthetics platform, profile is often free
  • Yelp — still heavily used for local discovery
  • Healthgrades — important if you have licensed medical providers
  • Zocdoc — if you offer medical consultations
  • Facebook Business Page — reviews + local visibility

Consistency is critical: your name, address, and phone must be identical across every platform. "Suite 200" vs "#200" vs no suite designation can create NAP inconsistencies that hurt your local ranking.

What This Looks Like in Practice

A med spa client of ours in Montana came to us ranking in positions 5–8 for their core services. Within 90 days of implementing these exact strategies — GBP optimization, service page expansion, a structured review generation process, and citation cleanup — they were in the top 3 for 12 of their 15 target services.

The combination of GBP completeness, fresh content, and review velocity creates a compounding effect. Google's local algorithm rewards activity, authority, and relevance — and when all three improve simultaneously, rankings move fast.
